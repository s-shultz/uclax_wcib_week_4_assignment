How We Got Started. This Is Our Story.

In 2011, Karen Shultz was diagnosed with Celiac Disease. She, along with her brother and sister, discovered that the Celiac gene ran in their family. Their children, seven out of nine, inherited the gene. As they learned to adapt to their new lifestyle, Karen grew frustrated by the slim offering for Celiac. She and her daughters worked to adapt their favorite recipes. This is how Mama Shultz's began.

What It's Like to be Celiac.

“Are you sure you don’t want a donut?” Trust me, I’m sure—I’m celiac. Being celiac means that I can't eat anything with gluten (a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley) or my body will respond by attacking my small intestine.
